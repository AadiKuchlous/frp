FAQ

Beneficiary FAQs:

1. Am I eligible to raise money for books?

If you are an individual or an organisation that works with children in India, and seek help to spread the joy of reading to these children, then you are eligible! You do not necessarily have to be a registered charity to build a campaign.

2. How long does it take to set up?

You will have to fill our 4 step form which will help us identify you and your work. the campaign is created, it will take us 2 to 3 working days to verify it. Once verified, your campaign goes live, and you can start spreading the word about your appeal.

3. How much does it cost?

There is no fees for setting up a Campaign. You can set up your Campaign for free and get donations for the books you require.

4. How long does a Campaign run for?

The average duration of a Campaign is 25 days but if you need more time, you can extend your campaign for a period of extra 5 days

5. What if I don’t achieve my set target?

Even if you don’t achieve 100% of your target, you are eligible to get books for whatever amount you have already raised. For example, if your target amount for Rs.10,000/- but at the end of 25/30 days you could raise only Rs.7500/-, you can still choose books for that amount. Books worth Rs.7500/- would reach you.

6. In how many days will I get the books?

After your campaign is over, a list of in-stock books is sent to you. You are required to revert within 10 working days with your choice and preference of books required. As soon as we get your list, the books will reach you within 30 working days.

7. What are the other things I should know before I start?

Do look at our Terms of Use before you start. If there are still any more questions, do drop in an email at donateabook@prathambooks.org.

Guidelines

Dos

  1. Every Campaign must have a project page with an image and description that introduces you/ your organization and the work it does. Choose an image which is a reflection of your work and makes your page visually engaging.
  2. State the purpose of the campaign clearly. E.g : Creating a Library for under-resourced school in rural Maharashtra.
  3. Tell the story behind your work. List achievements of the past and the expansion plans that you may have for future. Donors would feel more involved in your project if they know more about you.
  4. Have a target for your campaign — even if it’s just a rough one. A target is proof that you’ve planned for the number of books and hence know what are you going to do with it. This brings in credibility.
  5. Keep your language simple and descriptions within word limits but be as expressive as you can. The Donors would invest their money on your project if they know and trust you.

Donts

  1. Do Not share any false information. All information that you mention in your campaign is your responsibility and must be 100% true.
  2. Do not use any image / video / sound byte that does not belong to you and could be copyrighted. This could bring in legal issues that you would not want to cope up with.
  3. Do not put a target which is too much or too less . Putting a realistic target, with a clearly defined goal will help you achieve it.
  4. Refrain from using any text, image or language that may be offensive or obscene in nature. This could lead to your Campaign getting rejected at the review stage.
  5. Do not spam : This includes link-bombing, promoting a project where it shouldn’t be promoted, sending unsolicited @’s on Twitter, hyping third-party services, and using email lists from outside sources, to name a few.
